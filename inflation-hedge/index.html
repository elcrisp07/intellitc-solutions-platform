Historical Context

From 1970 to 1982 — a period that included severe stagflation — the median American home appreciated by 159%, exactly matching CPI inflation. Combined with the leverage benefit of a fixed-rate mortgage, homeowners preserved purchasing power. However, real (inflation-adjusted) gains were minimal during stagflation, and mortgage rates exceeding 16% crushed affordability and sales volume. The type of inflation matters as much as the rate.
