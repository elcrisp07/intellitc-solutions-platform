EV Market Insights

Key factors shaping EV charging investment returns

30C Federal Tax Credit

The Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit covers 30% of charger costs — up to $1,000 for residential and $100,000 for commercial installations. Scheduled through June 30, 2026.

15-30% Commercial ROI

Well-located commercial EV chargers generate 15-30% annual ROI with payback periods of 3-5 years, driven by the spread between electricity cost and charging price.

Property Value Uplift

Properties with EV charging typically see a 3-5% value premium. For Airbnb hosts, ChargePoint data shows up to 36% savings on charger hardware through partnership programs.

EV Adoption Accelerating

EV sales represent over 9% of new car sales in the US and growing. RV parks and vacation rentals with charging see increased bookings as EV travelers prioritize charging access.

L2 vs DC Fast Economics

Level 2 chargers ($900-$2,500 per port) offer lower upfront costs and reliable returns. DC Fast Chargers ($50,000-$250,000+) serve high-traffic locations with premium pricing ($0.35-$0.55/kWh).

Network Effects

Networked chargers with software platforms (~$20/month/port) enable payment processing, usage tracking, and load management — essential for commercial, RV, and Airbnb deployments.
